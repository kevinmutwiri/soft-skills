# Design Handover Process

> [home](./README.md)

At some point in your career, you’ll likely work with product designers or UI/UX designers to craft user journeys for various products you’re going to help build. Some of you will have technical roles in engineering and some may not– but regardless, it’s good practice to ensure that you understand the problem being solved. Being fully immersed in the project during the design phases can help you do this.

In some teams, engineers are not as involved in the early development (or ideation) phases of a project. Because of this, it’s important to have a seamless handover process of wireframes/prototypes/mock-ups (also known as design assets) to ensure nothing slips through the cracks. In this section, we’ll explore that process and learn the best practices for handing over designs from design (what your team has done) to engineers (who will do the deeper tech work to make your vision come to life). Excited?

[This video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PK8dLpwSqbw) describes how a lot more goes into handing over design work to the engineering team than just dropping a link into Slack. As you watch the video, pay attention to the following points in particular:

- Pay close attention to how the speaker uses titles between the different workflows to show where each workflow is supposed to go. Very hard to miss. Right?
- Notice how their team has project statuses built onto Figma so that other designers collaborating on this project can see the status of a certain workflow, whether blocked, in progress, or complete.
- Notice how all relevant documents that speak to the design are linked in one place to help anyone whom this design is shared with to understand the design context.

## Best Practices When Conducting Design Hand-Overs

1. Clear Documentation

Document design decisions, rationale, and specifications to provide developers with a comprehensive understanding of the design intent. This documentation should cover aspects such as color schemes, typography, spacing, interactive states, and any specific guidelines. For example, imagine you’re working on a mobile app design and need to hand it over to the development team. Alongside visual assets, you provide a detailed style guide with specific instructions for the implementation of micro-interactions and transitions, ensuring a consistent user experience.

2. Collaborative Communication

Establish open lines of communication with the development team to address any questions or concerns they may have during the handover process. Encourage feedback and clarification to foster a collaborative environment. Example: While handing over a complex web design project, you schedule a meeting with the development team to go through the design components together. This interactive session allows for clarification on design interactions, breakpoints, and responsive behavior, resulting in a shared understanding between the teams.

3. Contextual Explanations

Provide context around design decisions by explaining the user goals, personas, and user flows. Remember the notes left by the designer in the video we covered in the previous lesson? This information helps developers make informed decisions during the implementation phase. Example: In the design handover for a new e-commerce website, you share user research findings and highlight key pain points during the purchasing journey. By understanding the context, the development team can focus on optimizing those pain points, ultimately improving the user experience.

4. Use Standardized Formats

Adopt standardized formats such as design specifications, style guides, and design systems to streamline the handover process. This ensures consistency and reduces the chances of misinterpretation. Now, chances are that your company or team is going to have this defined for the team for ease of collaboration. In cases where there’s not an agreed-upon format, take leadership and seek guidance on how that can be established for the team. It provides a template for future use cases. Neat, right?

5. Emphasize Clarity and Simplicity

Keep design handovers clear, concise, and visually appealing. Use visual aids, annotations, and interactive prototypes to enhance understanding and reduce potential confusion.

6. Iterate and Learn

Continuously improve the design handover process based on feedback and lessons learned from previous projects. Remember retrospectives? Well, you can do the same for handover processes to ensure improvement over time. Encourage an open dialogue between designers and developers to foster a culture of continuous improvement.
