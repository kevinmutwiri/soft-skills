# Design Handover Process

> [home](./README.md)

At some point in your career, you’ll likely work with product designers or UI/UX designers to craft user journeys for various products you’re going to help build. Some of you will have technical roles in engineering and some may not– but regardless, it’s good practice to ensure that you understand the problem being solved. Being fully immersed in the project during the design phases can help you do this.

In some teams, engineers are not as involved in the early development (or ideation) phases of a project. Because of this, it’s important to have a seamless handover process of wireframes/prototypes/mock-ups (also known as design assets) to ensure nothing slips through the cracks. In this section, we’ll explore that process and learn the best practices for handing over designs from design (what your team has done) to engineers (who will do the deeper tech work to make your vision come to life). Excited?

[This video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PK8dLpwSqbw) describes how a lot more goes into handing over design work to the engineering team than just dropping a link into Slack. As you watch the video, pay attention to the following points in particular:

- Pay close attention to how the speaker uses titles between the different workflows to show where each workflow is supposed to go. Very hard to miss. Right?
- Notice how their team has project statuses built onto Figma so that other designers collaborating on this project can see the status of a certain workflow, whether blocked, in progress, or complete.
- Notice how all relevant documents that speak to the design are linked in one place to help anyone whom this design is shared with to understand the design context.
