**Main objective of the course**: _to equip you with key workspace skills encapsulated as 8 meta-skills_.

The meta skills and the skills that are part of that category are as follows:

## 8 Meta skills

### **Critical Thinking**

**Definition:** Evaluating information through research and analysis to form reasoned judgments and synthesize new insights.

* **Authentic Inquiry:** Formulating deep, meaningful questions.
* **Research:** Systematically gathering information.
* **Analysis:** Breaking down complex information into parts.
* **Argument & Judgement:** Forming logical conclusions and evaluations.
* **Synthesis:** Combining disparate elements into a coherent whole.

---

### **Leading Self**

**Definition:** Mastering personal growth through self-awareness, emotional regulation, and a commitment to ethical integrity.

* **Self Awareness:** Understanding one's own character and feelings.
* **Self Regulation:** Managing internal states and impulses.
* **Ethics & Integrity:** Adhering to moral principles and honesty.
* **Self Improvement:** Actively seeking to enhance one's skills and qualities.

---

### **Leading Others**

**Definition:** Building influential relationships and fostering collaboration through empathy and constructive feedback.

* **Empathy:** Understanding and sharing the feelings of others.
* **Feedback:** Giving and receiving evaluative information.
* **Relationship Building:** Establishing meaningful professional connections.
* **Influence:** Affecting the character or behavior of others.
* **Collaboration:** Working jointly with others toward a common goal.

---

### **Entrepreneurial Thinking**

**Definition:** Identifying market or social opportunities to create sustainable, iterative solutions.

* **Identifying Opportunities:** Recognizing potential for new ventures or improvements.
* **Creating Solutions:** Developing methods to solve specific problems.
* **Continuous Iteration:** Repeatedly refining products or processes.
* **Sustainability:** Ensuring solutions are viable over the long term.

---

### **Communicating for Impact**

**Definition:** Using storytelling, voice, and structured organization to effectively reach and move a specific audience.

* **Audience Awareness:** Tailoring messages to specific recipients.
* **Voice:** Expressing a distinct and effective persona.
* **Writing Process:** Using structured steps to produce text.
* **Organizing for Effective Communication:** Structuring information for maximum clarity.
* **Storytelling & Presentation:** Using narrative and visuals to engage others.

---

### **Managing Complex Tasks**

**Definition:** Defining problems and prioritizing resources to execute structured work plans efficiently.

* **Define the Problem:** Clearly identifying the core issue to be solved.
* **Structuring:** Organizing the components of a project.
* **Work Planning & Resource Allocation:** Assigning time and assets to tasks.
* **Prioritization:** Determining the order of importance for tasks.

---

### **Quantitative Reasoning**

**Definition:** Applying data modeling, empirical research, and mathematical logic to solve real-world problems.

* **Data Contextualization:** Understanding the background and meaning of data points.
* **Uncertainty & Modelling in the Real World:** Creating representations of complex, unpredictable systems.
* **Empirical Research:** Gaining knowledge by means of direct observation or experience.
* **Data Based Decision Making:** Using facts and figures to guide actions.
* **Quantitative Problem Solving:** Using mathematical skills to find solutions.

---

### **Tech Ecosystem**

**Definition:** Understanding technical literacy, tools, and ethics to work effectively within product and technical teams.

* **Working in Technical & Product Teams:** Collaborating with developers and designers.
* **Tech Tools:** Utilizing software and hardware effectively.
* **Ethics in Tech:** Navigating moral issues in digital environments.
* **Technical Literacy:** Fluency in understanding and using technology.

## What Makes The Professional Foundations Unique?

The Professional Foundations Course will equip you with **key tools**, and **critical soft and technical skills**. You will also invest your time in:

- Discovering who you are.
- Developing your character.
- Forming healthy lifestyle habits.
- Exploring rewarding ways to merge your professional aspirations and mission.

We will empower you to skillfully navigate and confidently lead the different aspects of your day-to-day.

This course will unlock your ability to build and practice additional critical real-world skills that are highly sought after by employers and clients.

As a Professional Foundations graduate, you will be:
- self-aware,
- aligned with your values,
- a great communicator and collaborator,
- humble and empathetic, and
- an analytical and creative thinker.

You will be well-equipped for your job search, as you will hold insider secrets for successfully finding and creating work opportunities and standing out in job interviews.

As part of this course, you will become a member of a community. Within that community, you will belong to a smaller group called a Squad, which consists of other peers who are also going through the course.

As Squad members, you will:

- Regularly communicate via eHub.
- Offer peer coaching sessions to one another.
- Support and motivate one another.

Out of the Squads, smaller teams are formed that collaborate on group projects (in-person or virtually). Many past participants report that the connections they made in their Squads and teams were what enabled them to complete the course successfully. - Milestones simulate projects done in the workplace, and provide you with actual work samples.
