## BUILD Framework

watch [this video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hhGO9maXDQA) that explains the BUILD framework while providing a real-life example

at the heart of Human Centered Design – and the BUILD process – is a problem-solving approach known as Design Thinking.

Design Thinking provides a way to solve complex problems by understanding human needs, reframing the issue in a human-centric way, generating multiple ideas through brainstorming and creative thinking, and testing out ideas through prototyping and innovation.

The five stages of design thinking are **Empathize**, **Define**, **Ideate**, **Prototype**, **Test**.

<img width="1920" height="1080" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/7923ed29-0b8c-4ef1-aa41-e4c6b51a51f3" />

Which stages of Design thinking have you already completed?

Throughout Phase 1 and your first 2 weeks of Phase 2 you have Empathized with your user, Defined your problem and Ideated possible solutions. You have now reached the Prototyping stage where you bring your solution idea to life. Isn’t this exciting?!

Looking at this from the human centered design perspective, prototyping forms part of ideating as this is where you create your solution and develop several iterations. From a BUILD framework approach, this is now the Inventing stage where you create a solution for your users. Both approaches allude to the same process, but the Build Process breaks down the steps further.

Something to think about:

What are the core principles / foundational beliefs of design thinking, human centered design and BUILD? Why are these so important?


They all place empathy and understanding the user at the core when defining the problem, ideating and creating the solution.

## User Personas

Your users are the central factor to your solution and invention, and what you propose must work for them. That means you need to understand who they are so you can create something they can use.

Creating a high-tech internet-heavy solution for a 97-year-old citizen in a rural community might not reflect a good understanding of your user.

a user persona can be a semi-fictional character based on your current or ideal user. It consists of a name, occupation, information, demographics, a personal story, pain points, and challenges.
