## User/Customer Segmentation

Customer segmentation is a powerful technique that allows businesses to group their customers, or users, based on shared characteristics.

Understanding the diverse needs and preferences of your users is crucial for effective marketing and tailored messaging. By paying attention to customer or user data, we can create customer segments based on several factors relevant to the problem being solved. See how all the pieces are connecting to form the big picture. This is why most times you will see customers refer to these insights as Know Your Customer (KYC).

Customer segmentation helps you identify distinct groups within your audience, enabling you to create targeted strategies and deliver personalized experiences. So, let’s dive into the world of customer segmentation and explore how it can unlock the marketing potential of your business.

For example, given the case of the company Spotify, users or customers can be segmented based on their engagement and discovery behavior. For instance, they can identify segments of users who actively explore new artists, genres, or curated playlists versus users who primarily stick to their familiar favorites. This segmentation can help Spotify deliver personalized recommendations to encourage music discovery or provide familiar listening to specific customers.

### What are some of the ways you would segment users or customers for your GCGO area?

Imagine you run an online clothing store catering to various age groups. Would a one-size-fits-all marketing approach be effective? Probably not.

#### Targeted Marketing

Customer segmentation allows you to divide your audience into groups based on factors such as demographics, interests, buying behavior, or psychographics. By understanding the unique needs and preferences of each segment, you can tailor your marketing campaigns to resonate with them effectively. For example, if you discover that one segment consists of environmentally conscious consumers, you can emphasize sustainable and eco-friendly aspects in your messaging.

#### Enhanced Customer Experience

Segmenting your customer base helps you create personalized experiences. By understanding the characteristics and preferences of each segment, you can customize your offerings, promotions, and communication to match their specific needs. This personalized approach makes customers feel valued and understood, ultimately fostering loyalty and customer satisfaction.

#### Optimal Resource Allocation

Customer segmentation enables you to allocate your resources more efficiently. By identifying your most profitable segments, you can prioritize marketing efforts, product development, and customer service initiatives for those groups. This focus helps maximize your return on investment and improve overall business performance.

## Size of The Opportunity 1

We have spent a good amount of time understanding our users and determining how a chosen problem affects them. But the best changemakers are able to view a problem or opportunity from both a micro lens (the effect on an individual person) and a macro lens (the effect on society as a whole).

Up until now, you have been looking at your chosen problem mostly on its own. But we need to remember that these problems do not exist in a vacuum. We need to balance the impact on individuals with scale for any business/solution we create.

### Why is determining the size of the opportunity important?

There are a few reasons why it is important to understand the size of the opportunity you are addressing:

- To convince investors that there is a lucrative business behind your idea
- To demonstrate the potential impact of the solution/business you are creating
- To determine the best approach or model to use
- To get the buy-in of team members or colleagues
- To determine the potential size of the GCGO you are tackling, you can calculate the TAM , or the “Total Addressable Market” and the SAM, or the “Serviceable Addressable Market”.


What is TAM?
The “Total Addressable Market” can be defined as:


- The total number of people impacted by the problem you have identified
- The total number of people who could benefit from your solution
- The total number of people who could leverage/use your solution in their daily lives

## Size of The Opportunity 2

**What is SAM?**
While the “TAM” gives you the whole potential “pie” of people that you can reach with your business/solution, the “Serviceable Addressable Market” focuses on the more realistic set of people that you can potentially serve.

There are a few ways that you can get from TAM to SAM:

- Assume what % of the TAM you can realistically penetrate
- Research how many customers businesses/solutions similar to yours are currently reaching
- Define more strictly the characteristics of the population you are trying to serve
