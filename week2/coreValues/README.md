## Core Values Experiential - Introduction

When you can identify the value of your actions, you will start to be deliberate about which actions are positive and have a purpose, and which actions you can work on because they are negative and take away from your purpose.

These simple experiential exercises will show you how to identify one of your core values, and can be extended to find other positive values you want to adopt and help you identify traits that you want to get rid of.

Steps 1 to 3 below outline what you will need to do.

<img width="740" height="727" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/84980ae9-4de9-4e91-b15a-78ec2a73e03d" />

* **Step 1:** Reflect on one meaningful life event.
* **Step 2:** Identify the values that you used in that event.
* **Step 3:** Repeat this exercise to see which values were missing.
* **The Goal:** This exercise is designed to help you reflect and unearth your **real** values.

To help you get started with this important thought process, we want you to think back and reflect on one happy action or moment of success. Here are three examples to help you understand what we mean:

- A victory playing in a sports team.
- Falling in love.
- Joining a yoga class, running group, or self-improvement club.

**Important**! Your action or moment of success should be associated with a value. For example:

- Winning the basketball tournament could show the value of resilience and/or teamwork.
- Falling in love could show the value of love, trust, and/or courage.
- Joining a yoga class could show the value of adventure and/or commitment.
